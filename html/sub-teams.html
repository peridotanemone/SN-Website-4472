Sub-teams

Programming

Four note autonomous routine from Crescendo, 2024

The programming sub-team brings the robot to life. With tools like Java, WPILibs, and Pathplanner, we create everything that is software-related. Our work includes building autonomous routines, designing control systems for different mechanisms, and figuring out logic ranging from operator controls to sensor/motor utilization and driver assistance.

Electrical

Showcasing our robot's LED capability

The electrical sub-team bridges the gap between mechanical and programming. We kick off the season by meticulously preparing materials and strategizing wiring plans. Our core responsibilities include hands-on tasks like soldering components, crimping connections, and creating a robust system. Beyond that, we focus on maintaining and reinforcing the robot's connections throughout the season.

Mechanical

Christian patches up the robot between matches

The mechanical sub-team is responsible for building, fixing and maintaining the robot throughout the season. Our job looks simple, but its complexity increases as the season progresses. We learn computer aided design (CAD), manufacture using industry-grade machines, and apply constructing principles. We also build the field elements out of wood in the beginning of the season. Between matches at competition, we deploy a pit crew to quickly repair any damage between matches.

Drive Team

Dominic, Chris, and Carlos prepare for a match

The drive team maneuvers the robot to victory. Driving the robot requires focus, strategy, and attention to environment. We represent our team at competition and bring the season's efforts to fruition. In addition, our human player takes on different tasks in every game, ranging from feeding the robot game pieces to controlling their own game piece to score.

Safety

Eva wins the Safety All-Star Award, 2024

The safety sub-team takes precautions so we can all leave with our heads and limbs attached to our bodies. We lead briefings on team etiquette, standard practices, and safety in the workshop. We also maintain a tidy pit area at competitions, in addition to handling injuries, emergencies, and equipment maintenance. We coordinate with coaches and adult mentors to ensure a safe working environment, physically and mentally.

Business

Valyn and Cheyanne talk business

The business sub-team handles all financial projects to fund our team's competitive edge for innovation. By setting up fundraisers, managing sponsorships, and partnering with local corporations, we generate revenue for our team. We also contribute to social media, including our team's Instagram, YouTube, Twitter, Facebook, and LinkedIn. We also handle photography and videography. We created this very site from scratch.

Data Science

Avery (left) and Jacob (right) discuss

The data science sub-team is responsible for the numerical data of the competitions. Whether it involves ourselves or other teams, we work closely with the Scouting and Strategy sub-teams to create and publish match data for competitions and determine the playing strength of teams. To do this, we use Python code to access data from The Blue Alliance's database once competitions begin in order to gain insight into teams we might have to play against in our competitions. We also use data from the Scouting sub-team to analyze teams mid-competition.
