Our Story

Our team settles in at Woodbridge HS, 2017

Beginnings (2013-2016)

In 2013, two students approached the Osbourn Park High School FRC (2068) coach about starting a county-wide FRC team. Thus team 4472, “SuperNOVA,” was born — a small, family-sized team consisting of students from all around Prince William County, Virginia. Our very first year competing, we won the Rookie All-Star award at the FRC World Championships.

However, over the next few years, we faced major challenges: the coaches responsible for our creation left, leaving us without school-affiliated mentors for 5 years. During this time, 2 highly dedicated volunteer mentors stepped up; SuperNOVA would not be here without them. Transportation made it difficult to recruit since most of our members lived 30+ minutes away from Osbourn Park High School, our program’s then home.

In 2017, we were left with 2 mentors and 5 students, who through grit and determination, persevered against the idea of merging with the 2068 Metal Jackets.

Move to Woodbridge Sr. High School (2017-2018)

During the summer of 2017, Mr. Carlos Castro, an experienced PLTW Engineering teacher and VEX Robotics coach, expressed interest in hosting SuperNOVA at Woodbridge Sr. High School. Thanks to him, we gained a space that was ours. Students in the southern and eastern part of Prince William County, who would otherwise be unable to participate in FIRST, had easier access to our practice space.

In Power Up , we pieced together a robot with a 50-year-old horizontal band saw and drill press. Despite this, we won our first blue banner in team history through grit.

Current Day (2018-now)

Since then, our team has blossomed. We now have over 40 members and a long list of dedicated mentors — including alumni — who love our team and our mission. Learning new techniques and technologies has allowed us to upgrade our machining capabilities, making us a better team in every season. We learn from our victories and our setbacks. We demonstrate what it means to love science, engineering, mathematics, and technology. Because that's what SuperNOVA is: a place where anyone can innovate, think, and inspire.

SuperNOVA is thriving once more, and we are thrilled to see where the future will take us.
